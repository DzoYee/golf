# Statics → Dynamics: How Grip Setup Influences Swing Motion

[← Connections](INDEX.md) · [← Grip](../INDEX.md)

This file is the bridge between the [statics](../statics/INDEX.md) and [dynamics](../dynamics/INDEX.md) sections. It maps each static grip variable to the dynamic outcomes it influences, making the cause-and-effect chains explicit.

## The Core Principle

The static grip doesn't cause the swing motion — the body does. But the grip sets the **starting conditions** that make certain motions easier, harder, or unnecessary. Changing the grip changes the difficulty level of every dynamic task in the swing.

## Mapping: Grip Strength → Dynamics

### Left Hand Strength

| Left Hand Position | [Forearm Rotation](../dynamics/forearm-rotation.md) | [Face Control](../dynamics/face-control.md) | [Low Point](../dynamics/low-point-control.md) |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Weak](../statics/weak.md) | High demand — must rotate aggressively to square | Face biased open — needs active closing | May release early to square face, moving low point back |
| [Neutral](../statics/neutral.md) | Moderate demand — natural rotation works | Face near baseline — body rotation determines angle | Low point depends on body mechanics |
| [Strong](../statics/strong.md) | Low demand — may need to resist rotation to prevent closing | Face biased closed — less active closing needed | Can maintain lag longer → supports forward low point |

### Right Hand Strength

| Right Hand Position | [Forearm Rotation](../dynamics/forearm-rotation.md) | [Face Control](../dynamics/face-control.md) | [Low Point](../dynamics/low-point-control.md) |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Weak](../statics/weak.md) | Adds open-face bias — more total rotation needed | Right hand resists closure | Less right hand interference with release timing |
| [Neutral](../statics/neutral.md) | Neutral role — doesn't add or fight rotation | Right hand follows left hand's lead | Passive right hand supports consistent release |
| [Strong](../statics/strong.md) | Adds closed-face bias — right hand wants to close actively | Right hand may override left hand closure | Active right hand can cause early release |

### Combined Strengths

The [combinations matrix](../statics/combinations.md) details all 9 pairings. At the mapping level:

- **Matched strengths** (both weak, both neutral, both strong) — the hands work as a unit. The dynamic tendencies are amplified but consistent.
- **Left stronger than right** (e.g., strong–neutral) — the left hand leads the face bias, and the right hand follows. Generally the most controllable asymmetry.
- **Right stronger than left** (e.g., neutral–strong) — the right hand has more closing authority than the left, which can create a flippy release and inconsistent [face control](../dynamics/face-control.md).
- **Opposite extremes** (strong–weak or weak–strong) — the hands fight each other. Inconsistent face control and release timing.

## Mapping: Hand Placement → Dynamics

### Finger Grip vs. Palm Grip ([Left Hand](../statics/left-hand.md))

| Placement | Wrist Hinge | Lag Retention | Low Point |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|-----------|
| Fingers (correct) | Full range | Good — can maintain angles deep into downswing | Forward |
| Palm (incorrect) | Restricted | Poor — early release | Behind ball |

### Grip Pressure

| Pressure | Forearm Rotation | Wrist Mobility | Face Control |
|----------|-----------------|----------------|--------------|
| Light | Free rotation, full range | Good hinge/unhinge | Responsive — face reacts to body motion |
| Moderate | Natural rotation | Normal mobility | Predictable |
| Tight | Restricted rotation | Limited hinge | Rigid — face doesn't release naturally |

## The Decision Framework

When analyzing or changing a grip, work through this sequence:

1. **Identify the ball flight problem** — Is it a face issue (curves), a path issue (straight misses), or a contact issue (fat/thin)?

2. **If face issue** → Check grip strength. Map current grip to the face bias in the tables above. Adjust grip strength to change the bias.

3. **If path issue** → Check [left shoulder connection](left-shoulder.md). The grip may be creating a shoulder position that encourages the wrong path.

4. **If contact issue** → Check hand placement (fingers vs. palm), grip pressure, and right hand dominance. These affect [low point control](../dynamics/low-point-control.md).

5. **Change one variable at a time** — Adjust left hand strength first (it has the most influence), then right hand, then pressure. Moving multiple variables simultaneously makes it impossible to isolate cause and effect.
