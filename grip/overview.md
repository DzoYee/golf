# Grip Overview

[← Grip](INDEX.md) · [← Home](../README.md)

## What Is Grip?

Grip refers to how the hands are placed on the club and how they function during the swing. It's the foundation of club control — the hands are the only interface between the golfer's body and the club, so every force, rotation, and correction passes through the grip.

## Why It Matters

Grip affects three things simultaneously:

1. **Clubface orientation at impact** — The angle of the face when it meets the ball is the single largest factor in shot direction. Grip strength (how rotated the hands are on the club) directly sets the baseline face angle. See [face control](dynamics/face-control.md).

2. **Forearm rotation through the swing** — A strong grip requires less active rotation to square the face; a weak grip demands more. The grip sets the "rotation budget" for the swing. See [forearm rotation](dynamics/forearm-rotation.md).

3. **Connection to the body** — How the left hand sits on the club affects the left shoulder's position at address and through the swing, which cascades into swing plane and path. See [left shoulder connection](connections/left-shoulder.md).

## How to Read This Section

The grip section is organized into three areas:

- **[Statics](statics/INDEX.md)** — The positions: where each hand goes, what "strong," "neutral," and "weak" mean, and how left and right hand strengths combine into 9 possible configurations.
- **[Dynamics](dynamics/INDEX.md)** — The motions: what happens to the forearms, clubface, and ground contact during the swing, and how the static grip setup influences those motions.
- **[Connections](connections/INDEX.md)** — The ripple effects: how grip decisions connect to the shoulders, swing plane, and the rest of the body.

Start with statics to understand the vocabulary, then move to dynamics to see how those positions play out in motion, then connections to see the full picture.
