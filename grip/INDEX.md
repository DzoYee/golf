# Grip

[← Home](../README.md)

The grip is the only point of contact between the golfer and the club. Everything the body does to move the club must pass through the hands. This section covers how the hands are placed on the club (statics), what happens during the swing (dynamics), and how grip choices ripple into the rest of the swing (connections).

- A [strong left hand](statics/strong.md) tends to promote a closed face at impact → see [face control](dynamics/face-control.md)
- [Forearm rotation](dynamics/forearm-rotation.md) is partly dictated by where the hands start → see [combinations](statics/combinations.md)
- The [left hand](statics/left-hand.md) connects mechanically to the [left shoulder](connections/left-shoulder.md), influencing swing plane
