# Face Control

[← Dynamics](INDEX.md) · [← Grip](../INDEX.md)

Face control is the ability to deliver the clubface at a predictable angle at impact. The face angle at the moment the club contacts the ball is the **dominant factor in starting direction** — roughly 75-85% of the ball's initial direction comes from where the face is pointing, not the path of the swing.

## The Three Face Positions at Impact

| Face Position | Description | Ball Flight Effect |
|---------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Open** | Face points right of the swing path (for a right-handed golfer) | Ball starts right of path, curves right (fade/slice) |
| **Square** | Face points along the swing path | Ball starts on the path line, minimal curve |
| **Closed** | Face points left of the swing path | Ball starts left of path, curves left (draw/hook) |

"Open" and "closed" are relative to the **swing path**, not the target line. A face that's square to the target but open to the path will still curve.

## What Determines Face Angle

Face angle at impact is the result of several factors, but the grip is the starting point:

### 1. Grip Strength (Static Setup)

The [grip strength](../statics/INDEX.md) sets the baseline face angle before the swing begins:

- [Strong grip](../statics/strong.md) → face biased closed → less rotation needed to square it
- [Neutral grip](../statics/neutral.md) → face near baseline → moderate rotation needed
- [Weak grip](../statics/weak.md) → face biased open → more rotation needed to square it

This is the "pre-set" component. It's fixed once the golfer takes their grip.

### 2. Forearm Rotation (Dynamic Action)

[Forearm rotation](forearm-rotation.md) through the downswing closes the face. The rotation rate and timing determine whether the face arrives open, square, or closed relative to the path. The grip determines how much rotation is *needed* — the body determines how much rotation is *supplied*.

### 3. Wrist Conditions

The left wrist can be bowed (flexed), flat, or cupped (extended) at impact:

- **Bowed** — closes the face. Common with strong grips, reduces dynamic loft.
- **Flat** — neutral face influence. The "textbook" impact position.
- **Cupped** — opens the face. Adds dynamic loft, produces higher, weaker shots.

### 4. Right Hand Action

The [right hand](../statics/right-hand.md) can add or override face closure through the hitting zone. An overly active right hand "flipping" through impact is one of the most common causes of inconsistent face control — it adds a variable rotation source that's hard to time.

## Face Control and Grip Strength: The Core Relationship

The reason grip is the **first variable** teachers look at when diagnosing ball flight:

```
Strong grip  →  face starts closed  →  needs LESS rotation  →  easier to square or close
Neutral grip →  face starts neutral →  needs MODERATE rotation  →  requires consistent mechanics
Weak grip    →  face starts open    →  needs MORE rotation  →  harder to square, easier to leave open
```

This means **grip strength is the coarsest adjustment knob for face control**. Before changing swing mechanics, changing the grip can shift the entire face-control curve. See [combinations](../statics/combinations.md) for how the two hands' strengths interact.

## Common Face Control Problems

| Problem | Likely Cause | Grip Consideration |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Consistent slice | Face open to path at impact | Grip may be too weak — consider strengthening left hand |
| Consistent hook | Face closed to path at impact | Grip may be too strong — consider weakening left hand |
| Inconsistent direction | Face angle varies shot to shot | Hands may be at conflicting strengths, or right hand is overactive |
| Straight pulls (left) | Face square to path, but path is left | Grip may not be the issue — path problem |
| Straight pushes (right) | Face square to path, but path is right | Grip may not be the issue — path problem |

Note: pulls and pushes are **path** problems with good face control. Only curves (fade, draw, slice, hook) indicate a face-to-path mismatch where grip is the primary lever.
