# Low Point Control

[← Dynamics](INDEX.md) · [← Grip](../INDEX.md)

Low point is where the club reaches its lowest point in the swing arc — the bottom of the circle. Controlling where this happens relative to the ball is what separates clean contact from fat and thin shots. While low point is primarily a body and weight shift issue, the grip has a meaningful influence.

## What Low Point Means

For iron shots off the ground, the ideal low point is **slightly ahead of the ball** (toward the target). This means the club is still descending when it reaches the ball, contacts the ball first, then takes a divot after the ball.

| Low Point Position | Contact Quality | Divot |
|-------------------|----------------|-------|
| Behind the ball | Fat — club hits ground before ball | Behind the ball or no divot (bounced) |
| At the ball | Thin or slightly heavy | Right at the ball |
| Ahead of the ball | Clean, ball-first | After the ball (ideal) |

For driver and tee shots, the low point should be **behind the ball** because the ball is teed up and you want to catch it on the upswing.

## How Grip Affects Low Point

The grip influences low point through two mechanisms:

### 1. Wrist Angle Retention

A proper grip allows the golfer to maintain wrist angles (lag) deep into the downswing, which keeps the low point forward. Grip problems that affect this:

- **Too much in the palm** — A [left hand](../statics/left-hand.md) that grips the club through the palm instead of the fingers reduces wrist hinge range. Less hinge means less lag, which means the club releases early and the low point moves backward (behind the ball).
- **Too tight** — Excessive grip pressure in either hand restricts wrist mobility. The hands can't hinge and unhinge freely, which disrupts the natural release point.

### 2. Right Hand Dominance

The [right hand](../statics/right-hand.md) can influence low point by how it releases:

- **Early right hand release** — If the right hand fires too early (casting), it throws the clubhead outward and downward prematurely, moving the low point behind the ball. This is connected to grip strength — a very [strong right hand](../statics/strong.md) position can encourage the right hand to take over the release.
- **Right hand holds off** — When the right hand stays in its role (providing speed, not direction), the low point stays forward. A [neutral right hand](../statics/neutral.md) makes this passive role easier.

### 3. Forearm Rotation Connection

[Forearm rotation](forearm-rotation.md) and low point interact. When the forearms rotate excessively or at the wrong time, the low point can shift:

- **Early rotation** — Forearms rotate before the hands reach the ball, which can shallow the swing and move the low point back
- **Late rotation** — Forearms rotate after the hands pass the ball, keeping the low point forward but potentially leaving the face open

The grip's influence on forearm rotation timing indirectly affects low point. See [forearm rotation](forearm-rotation.md) for more detail.

## Low Point and Grip Strength

The relationship is indirect but real:

| Grip Setup | Tendency | Low Point Effect |
|------------|----------|-----------------|
| [Strong grip](../statics/strong.md) | Less rotation needed, face pre-closed | Golfer can maintain lag longer without worrying about face angle → tends to support forward low point |
| [Weak grip](../statics/weak.md) | More rotation needed to square face | Golfer may release early to square the face in time → can move low point back |
| [Neutral grip](../statics/neutral.md) | Moderate rotation demand | Low point depends more on body mechanics than grip |

This is a secondary effect — the primary driver of low point is the body's forward weight shift and rotation. But when a golfer changes their grip, they may notice changes in ground contact because the rotation demand has changed, and their release timing adjusts.

## Common Problems

| Problem | Symptom | Grip Check |
|---------|---------|------------|
| Consistently fat | Divots behind the ball | Is the club in the palm instead of fingers? Is the right hand too strong, causing early release? |
| Consistently thin | Topping or blading | Is the golfer flipping (early release from right hand dominance)? The rise after the flip creates thin contact. |
| Inconsistent contact | Mix of fat and thin | Grip pressure varying? Hands at conflicting strengths creating inconsistent release? |
