# Weak Grip

[← Statics](INDEX.md) · [← Grip](../INDEX.md)

A weak grip means the hand is rotated toward the target on the club. "Weak" is a positional term — it has nothing to do with grip pressure or hand strength. A grip can be weak in the [left hand](left-hand.md), the [right hand](right-hand.md), or both.

## What It Looks Like

**Left hand weak:**
- 0–1 knuckles visible at address (looking down)
- The V formed by the thumb and index finger points toward the left ear or left shoulder
- The left forearm is rotated slightly outward (supinated less than neutral)

**Right hand weak:**
- The palm faces more toward the ground
- The V formed by the thumb and index finger points toward the chin or left of it
- The right forearm sits more on top of the grip

## What It Does

A weak grip creates a starting position where the clubface is biased toward **open**. This has predictable downstream effects:

1. **More rotation needed to square the face** — Because the face starts in a more open position, the golfer needs more [forearm rotation](../dynamics/forearm-rotation.md) through the downswing to get the face square at impact. This increases the timing demand.

2. **Fade/slice tendency** — If the golfer doesn't add enough rotation, the face arrives at impact open, producing left-to-right curvature (fade or slice). See [face control](../dynamics/face-control.md).

3. **Higher ball flight** — An open face adds dynamic loft at impact, producing higher launch with more spin.

## When It's Used

- Players who naturally rotate the forearms aggressively may use a weak grip to prevent the face from closing too much
- Players who want to hit a controlled fade
- Some tour players (notably Ben Hogan in his later career) used a weak left hand to eliminate the left miss

## Tradeoffs

| Advantage | Disadvantage |
|-----------|--------------|
| Reduces hook tendency | Increases slice tendency if rotation is insufficient |
| Can produce a reliable fade | Requires more active forearm rotation — more timing-dependent |
| Higher ball flight for soft landings | Can cost distance due to higher spin and open face at impact |
