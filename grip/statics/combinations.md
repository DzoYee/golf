# Grip Strength Combinations

[← Statics](INDEX.md) · [← Grip](../INDEX.md)

The [left hand](left-hand.md) and [right hand](right-hand.md) each have three grip strength positions — [weak](weak.md), [neutral](neutral.md), and [strong](strong.md) — which combine into a 3×3 matrix of 9 possible pairings. Each combination produces a different face bias and rotation demand.

## The Matrix

| | **Left Weak** | **Left Neutral** | **Left Strong** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Right Weak** | [Weak–Weak](#weak-weak) | [Neutral–Weak](#neutral-weak) | [Strong–Weak](#strong-weak) |
| **Right Neutral** | [Weak–Neutral](#weak-neutral) | [Neutral–Neutral](#neutral-neutral) | [Strong–Neutral](#strong-neutral) |
| **Right Strong** | [Weak–Strong](#weak-strong) | [Neutral–Strong](#neutral-strong) | [Strong–Strong](#strong-strong) |

*Rows = right hand strength. Columns = left hand strength. Format: Left–Right.*

## Matched Combinations

These pairings have both hands at the same strength. The Vs formed by each hand point in roughly the same direction.

### Weak-Weak

Both hands rotated toward the target. Maximum open-face bias.

- **Face tendency:** Strongly open
- **Rotation demand:** High — requires aggressive [forearm rotation](../dynamics/forearm-rotation.md) to square the face
- **Ball flight:** High fade/slice tendency
- **Who uses it:** Rare. Players with extremely fast forearm rotation who need to hold the face open. Some players use this for specialty cut shots.
- **Risk:** Very timing-dependent. Miss is a high, weak fade or slice.

### Neutral-Neutral

Both hands in baseline position. No positional bias in either direction.

- **Face tendency:** Neutral — face angle determined entirely by body rotation
- **Rotation demand:** Moderate — natural rotation squares the face
- **Ball flight:** Straight, with curve direction determined by rotation consistency
- **Who uses it:** Common teaching starting point. Suits players with consistent, moderate forearm rotation.
- **Risk:** Doesn't mask any swing flaw. Whatever the body does shows up in the ball flight.

### Strong-Strong

Both hands rotated away from the target. Maximum closed-face bias.

- **Face tendency:** Strongly closed
- **Rotation demand:** Low — face is already pre-closed, so the golfer must actively [hold off rotation](../dynamics/forearm-rotation.md) to avoid hooking
- **Ball flight:** Low draw/hook tendency
- **Who uses it:** Players fighting a chronic slice, or those who want a stock low draw. Some long drive competitors use this for maximum release.
- **Risk:** Miss is a low hook. Difficult to hit fades. Can restrict wrist hinge and cost clubhead speed if too extreme.

## Mixed Combinations: Left Stronger Than Right

These pairings have the left hand rotated more than the right, creating an asymmetry.

### Strong-Weak

Left hand strong, right hand weak. The hands "fight" each other — the left hand wants to close the face while the right hand resists.

- **Face tendency:** Depends on which hand dominates through impact. The built-in tension can produce inconsistency.
- **Rotation demand:** Moderate but conflicted — the hands have different starting positions for rotation
- **Ball flight:** Unpredictable without a well-grooved release pattern
- **Who uses it:** Uncommon intentionally. Sometimes seen when a player has been given conflicting grip advice.
- **Risk:** Inconsistent face control. The internal conflict between the hands makes [face control](../dynamics/face-control.md) harder to repeat.

### Strong-Neutral

Left hand strong, right hand neutral. The left hand provides a closed-face bias while the right hand sits in a neutral role.

- **Face tendency:** Moderately closed — the left hand leads the bias
- **Rotation demand:** Low to moderate — the left hand does some of the closing work
- **Ball flight:** Draw tendency with moderate trajectory
- **Who uses it:** Very common on tour. This is arguably the most popular combination among elite players. The strong left hand provides a reliable face bias, and the neutral right hand allows feel and control.
- **Risk:** Miss is a pull or hook if left hand over-rotates. But generally very playable.

### Neutral-Weak

Left hand neutral, right hand weak. Mild version of the asymmetry — the left hand is only slightly stronger than the right.

- **Face tendency:** Mildly open — the right hand's weak position adds slight open-face bias
- **Rotation demand:** Moderate to slightly high
- **Ball flight:** Slight fade tendency
- **Who uses it:** Players who want a mild fade without committing to a fully weak grip
- **Risk:** Low risk. Mild bias that's easy to override.

## Mixed Combinations: Right Stronger Than Left

These pairings have the right hand rotated more than the left.

### Weak-Strong

Left hand weak, right hand strong. Opposite extremes — the right hand wants to close the face aggressively while the left hand is in a position that encourages it to stay open.

- **Face tendency:** Dominated by the right hand's closing action through impact. Can produce a "flippy" release.
- **Rotation demand:** Conflicted — the right hand needs to overpower the left
- **Ball flight:** Inconsistent. Tends toward hooks when the right hand wins, pushes when it loses.
- **Who uses it:** Rarely intentional. This combination often appears when a strong-handed player death-grips with the right hand.
- **Risk:** High. The internal conflict makes consistent contact and face control very difficult. Generally a combination to move away from.

### Neutral-Strong

Left hand neutral, right hand strong. The right hand provides a closed-face bias from underneath the club.

- **Face tendency:** Moderately closed — right hand biased
- **Rotation demand:** Low to moderate
- **Ball flight:** Draw tendency, but the right hand can become overactive
- **Who uses it:** Less common than strong-neutral. Some players prefer the feel of a strong right hand.
- **Risk:** Right hand dominance. When the right hand is stronger than the left, it can take over the release, making [face control](../dynamics/face-control.md) less consistent and reducing the left hand's role.

### Weak-Neutral

Left hand weak, right hand neutral. The left hand is weaker than baseline, creating mild open-face bias.

- **Face tendency:** Mildly to moderately open
- **Rotation demand:** Moderate to high
- **Ball flight:** Fade tendency
- **Who uses it:** Players who want to hit fades and prefer the right hand in a neutral, passive role
- **Risk:** Moderate. Requires sufficient rotation to square the face, but the neutral right hand won't fight it.

## How to Use This Matrix

1. **Diagnose current grip** — Count knuckles on the left hand, check the right hand palm position. Identify which cell you're in.
2. **Identify the ball flight** — Match the typical shot pattern to the combination's tendency.
3. **Choose a target combination** — If changing the grip, know which cell you're moving to and what it will change about [face control](../dynamics/face-control.md) and [rotation demand](../dynamics/forearm-rotation.md).
4. **Change one hand at a time** — Moving both hands simultaneously changes too many variables. Adjust the left hand first (it has more influence on face angle), then fine-tune with the right.
