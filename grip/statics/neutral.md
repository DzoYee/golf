# Neutral Grip

[← Statics](INDEX.md) · [← Grip](../INDEX.md)

A neutral grip places the hand in a baseline position on the club — not rotated toward the target and not rotated away from it. It's the reference point that [weak](weak.md) and [strong](strong.md) are defined relative to. A grip can be neutral in the [left hand](left-hand.md), the [right hand](right-hand.md), or both.

## What It Looks Like

**Left hand neutral:**
- 2 knuckles visible at address (looking down)
- The V formed by the thumb and index finger points toward the right ear or between the chin and right shoulder
- The left forearm sits in a natural, relaxed position — neither actively rotated in nor out

**Right hand neutral:**
- The palm faces roughly toward the target
- The V formed by the thumb and index finger points toward the right ear, roughly parallel to the left hand's V
- The lifeline sits snugly on the [left thumb](left-hand.md)

## What It Does

A neutral grip produces a starting position where the clubface has no strong bias toward open or closed. The downstream effects:

1. **Moderate rotation demand** — The golfer needs a natural, moderate amount of [forearm rotation](../dynamics/forearm-rotation.md) through the swing to square the face. Not as much as a weak grip requires, not as little as a strong grip allows.

2. **Straight shot as default** — With the right amount of rotation, the face arrives square at impact, producing a straight ball flight or a very slight curve. See [face control](../dynamics/face-control.md).

3. **Balanced ball flight** — Neither adding nor removing dynamic loft relative to the club's built-in loft.

## When It's Used

- A common starting point for instruction — many teachers build from neutral and adjust from there
- Players with a moderate, natural forearm rotation pattern
- Players who want the ability to work the ball both directions (fade and draw) without changing their grip

## Tradeoffs

| Advantage | Disadvantage |
|-----------|--------------|
| Balanced — no strong bias in either direction | Doesn't correct an existing rotation issue |
| Good starting point for learning | May not suit players with very fast or very slow forearm rotation |
| Allows both shot shapes | Requires consistent rotation to produce consistent results |

## The "Both Neutral" Setup

When both hands are neutral — the [neutral-neutral combination](combinations.md) — the grip imposes the least positional bias on the swing. This means the golfer's natural body rotation and forearm action determine the face angle at impact, with minimal help or resistance from the grip.

This is the most "transparent" grip: what the body does is what the face does. That's an advantage for skilled players with repeatable mechanics and a challenge for players whose body action is inconsistent.
